Classification metrics

Precision\( \dfrac{TP}{TP+FP} \) — of everything we flagged positive, how much truly is. "How often we cry wolf."
Recall\( \dfrac{TP}{TP+FN} \) — of all actual positives, how much we caught (a.k.a. sensitivity, TPR). "How much we miss."
PR-AUCArea under the precision-recall curve across thresholds. Best summary for imbalanced / rare-positive problems.
ROC-AUCArea under the ROC curve (TPR vs. FPR). = P(model ranks a random positive above a random negative). 0.5 = random, 1.0 = perfect. Can look optimistic under heavy imbalance → prefer PR-AUC there.

Regression metrics

RMSE\( \sqrt{\dfrac{1}{n}\sum (y-\hat y)^2} \) — root mean squared error. Same units as target. Range [0, ∞), 0 = perfect. Penalizes large errors most (outlier-sensitive).
MAE\( \dfrac{1}{n}\sum |y-\hat y| \) — mean absolute error. Same units. Range [0, ∞), 0 = perfect. Robust to outliers.
MAPE\( \dfrac{1}{n}\sum \left|\dfrac{y-\hat y}{y}\right| \) — mean absolute % error. Scale-free. Range [0, ∞) as a % (can exceed 100%), 0 = perfect. Blows up when y is near 0.
R²\( 1 - \dfrac{SS_{\text{res}}}{SS_{\text{tot}}} \) — share of variance explained. Range (−∞, 1]: 1 = perfect, 0 = no better than predicting the mean, negative = worse than the mean. Pair with an error metric — never report alone.

Probability

Bayes' theorem\( P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(B \mid A)\,P(A)}{P(B)} \) — updates the probability of hypothesis A given evidence B. Denominator: \( P(B) = P(B\mid A)P(A) + P(B\mid \neg A)P(\neg A) \).

Hypothesis testing

α (alpha)Significance level — the pre-set false-positive rate: \( P(\text{reject } H_0 \mid H_0\ \text{true}) \) = Type I error. Typically 0.05.
β (beta)False-negative rate: \( P(\text{fail to reject } H_0 \mid H_0\ \text{false}) \) = Type II error. Power \( = 1 - \beta \). Typically β = 0.20 → 80% power.
z-testCompares a mean when the population SD (σ) is known or n is large; normal distribution. \( Z = \dfrac{\bar X - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \)
X̄ = sample mean, μ = population mean, σ = population SD, n = sample size. Also the standard test for proportions.
t-testCompares a mean when the population SD is unknown (estimated by sample SD s) / small n; t-distribution (fatter tails). \( t = \dfrac{\bar X - \mu}{s/\sqrt{n}} \), df = \( n-1 \)
Same shape as z, but with sample SD s in place of σ.
